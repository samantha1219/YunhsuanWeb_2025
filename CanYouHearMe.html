“Can you hear me?”

Sound Installation

2025 audio system

Programming | YUN-HSUAN CHENG

Sound | Riya Lakshman

A sound installation

Technique

TouchDesigner micro-controller

CONCEPT

This installation employs a transparent cubic enclosure as a receptive field for light, trace, and vibration. Etched lines within the volume—reminiscent of waveforms or residual signals—remain latent until activated by an external light source, while low-frequency sound emanates from within the plinth, transmitting subtle vibrations through the structure. These elements render the work not only visible but palpable.

Sound operates here as a suppressed form of speech: present yet constrained, resonating through the base rather than fully emerging into the surrounding space. The interplay of light, inscription, and vibration foregrounds the instability of communication—how signals are transmitted, distorted, and only partially received.

While the transparent form suggests openness, the internal traces and muted sonic output resist full articulation, exposing a disjunction between expression and comprehension. By posing the question can you hear me?, the work reflects on the condition of contemporary communication, where voices persist in states of attenuation, caught between presence and erasure.

DOCUMENTATION

Sound detected on the moon drives ripples of water and light, giving voice to what was once suppressed, letting it breathe again in another form.
