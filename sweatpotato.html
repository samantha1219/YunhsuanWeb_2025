Still Singing

Video Installation with Sound

2025 Installation Music Video

Visual / Sound Production / Motion Capture | YUN-HSUAN CHENG

Music Co-written and Performed by Hannah Stubbs

Through a children's songs sung from the perspective of a sweet potato, this work discusses Taiwans legitimacy. Singing ceaselessly although it can't be heard, hidden underwater, in an abandoned amusement park, and alone in the forest.

Technologies

Unreal Engine Logic Pro X Motion Capture 3D Animation Sound Design

CONCEPT

In a silent amusement park, the Ferris wheel has stopped turning, but the sweet potato is still singing. It is neither a song of victory nor despair, only persistence. The potato will continue to sing for those who are ignored, underestimated, and undervalued. The sweet potato embodies the island, sharing its shape and its resilience. Children’s songs are simple, repetitive, and often nationally universal.

Although they serve as early childhood entertainment, they are coded with the morals and customs of our societies. Shaping our subconscious perception of what it means to be a good child and citizen. Taiwan’s mountains were once the sea, formed into jagged land by the collision of tectonic plates. The land itself is accustomed to the fluidity and transformation of existence.
